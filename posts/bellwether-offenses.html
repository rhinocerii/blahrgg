Lansing: What Bellwether Public Relations Has Done for Deep Green

March 26, 2026

LANSING, Mich. — Bellwether Public Relations is an 18-person firm in downtown Lansing. Its clients include the Michigan Health and Hospital Association, the Steelcase Foundation, Michigan Virtual, and the Early Childhood Investment Corporation. It has won six Silver Anvil Awards from the Public Relations Society of America. Its partners — Josh Hovey, Angela Minicuci, and Andie Poole — bought the firm from its founders in 2022 and rebranded it from Martin Waymire in October 2024.

Bellwether is also the paid communications consultant for Deep Green Technologies, a UK data center company seeking to buy city-owned land in Lansing for $1.4 million. Here is what the firm and its partner Josh Hovey have done on that engagement, based on public records.

Fabricated Public Support

Four waves of template emails supporting the Deep Green project were sent to the Lansing City Council through a digital advocacy platform called The Soft Edge. At least 36 letters were generated this way. In 23 of them, the listed sender's email address was the platform's own relay address, meaning the people named may never have written or approved what was sent in their names. At the March 23, 2026 public hearing, more than half of all written support for the project was platform-generated.

The campaign was run through the Lansing Regional Chamber of Commerce's advocacy infrastructure. Bellwether's connection to the Chamber is documented below.

PAC Committee Membership

Hovey is a committee member of the Lansing Regional Chamber PAC (LRC-PAC, MiTN Committee 000516). LRC-PAC endorsed six of the eight sitting City Council members who will vote on the Deep Green land sale. Hovey is also LRC-PAC's largest individual donor, contributing $4,810 over his lifetime — more than the PAC's own treasurer, Chamber CEO Tim Daman ($2,850).

The Lansing Regional Chamber of Commerce is the institutional advocate for the Deep Green project. Hovey bridges the project's paid advocacy and the political infrastructure that endorsed and funded the council majority.

Chamber Tax Reporting

The Lansing Regional Chamber of Commerce reports zero political expenditures on its IRS filings while LRC-PAC spends hundreds of thousands of dollars endorsing and funding candidates. The Chamber and the PAC share staff, leadership, and an address. Hovey sits on the PAC committee. The PAC does the political spending. The Chamber claims it doesn't.

False Government Credential

Bellwether's website states that Hovey "serves on" the Lansing Planning Commission. He does not. He left the commission and was replaced by Spencer Lippert. Lippert then voted to approve the Deep Green rezoning 5-2 on March 3, 2026. The false claim has been on Bellwether's website since at least May 2022, per archived versions of the site.

Controlled the Public Engagement

Bellwether hosted the Deep Green community information sessions, controlling what the public was told about the project before the council process began. The same firm that managed the community's first impression of the project also ran the fake letter campaign and sits on the PAC that funds the council.

Phone Campaign

Three days before the March 23 public hearing, Lansing residents received patch-through phone calls urging them to contact their council members in support of Deep Green. The campaign targeted residents whose council members were voting on the land sale. The timing aligned with WLNS reporting that the project had only five likely yes votes against a six-vote supermajority threshold.

Coalition Launch

In March 2026, a coalition called Michigan for Responsible Data Centers launched with coordinated media coverage, presenting itself as an independent source of information about data centers. The coalition has no disclosed funding source and no listed physical address. Its messaging, timing, and network connections track directly to the Deep Green advocacy campaign and the same Chamber-Bellwether infrastructure.

The Other Clients

Bellwether's public client list includes organizations whose work depends on public trust:

  • Michigan Health and Hospital Association
  • Michigan Association of Health Plans (MAHP Advocacy PAC, $1,278 in Bellwether printing in 2025)
  • Steelcase Foundation
  • Michigan Virtual
  • Michigan Future, Inc.
  • Michigan League for Public Policy
  • Early Childhood Investment Corporation
  • Launch Michigan
  • Hemlock Semiconductor

These organizations hired Bellwether to manage their public image. The practices documented above are what the firm does on other engagements.
